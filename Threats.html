Cybersecurity Threats

Become aware of the latest Cybersecurity threats. Here are some the most common ways that has been used to take advantage of organizations vulnerabilities.

Cyber Threat Awareness

Phishing Attacks

What is it? Phishing is a cyberattack where hackers trick users into giving up personal or financial information through fake emails or websites.

Example: In 2020, Twitter suffered a major phishing attack that compromised employee accounts and led to fake tweets promoting a Bitcoin scam.

Weakness: Employees were not trained to recognize social engineering and clicked on fake login pages.

Prevention Tips:

  • Train users to identify fake emails and suspicious links.
  • Use two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts.
  • Monitor employee access and limit admin privileges.

Malware Infections

What is it? Malware is malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Example: The WannaCry ransomware attack in 2017 affected over 200,000 computers worldwide by encrypting data and demanding ransom payments in Bitcoin.

Weakness: Many systems hadn’t applied a critical Microsoft security patch (EternalBlue vulnerability).

Prevention Tips:

  • Keep systems and software updated with security patches.
  • Use reliable antivirus and anti-malware tools.
  • Regularly back up important data and store it offline.

Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks

What is it? A DoS attack floods a system or network with traffic to crash or slow it down, making it unavailable to users.

Example: GitHub was hit with a record-breaking 1.35 Tbps DDoS attack in 2018, temporarily knocking the site offline.

Weakness: The attackers exploited vulnerable Memcached servers to amplify traffic.

Prevention Tips:

  • Use cloud-based DDoS protection (e.g., Cloudflare, AWS Shield).
  • Configure firewalls and rate-limiting to block excessive traffic.
  • Monitor network activity for unusual patterns or surges.
